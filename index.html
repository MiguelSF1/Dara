Game rules

Board and pieces

Dara is played on a board with 6 rows x 5 columns Other dimensions are possible, such as 6 x 6 Each of the 2 players has 12 pieces One player plays with black squares, another with white All 22 pieces are always visible, on the board or outside Pieces outside the board are grouped by color, on each side

Basic rules

Players choose a color and decide who goes first Initially the board is empty The game has 2 phases: Phase 1 - Placing the pieces (drop) Phase 2 - Move the pieces (move)

Pieces in line

They are considered online sequences of 3 or more contiguous pieces horizontally or vertically (not diagonally) all the same color

Putting the pieces

Players alternately place one of their pieces on a free square No more than 3 pieces can be placed in a row There can be 3 pieces in a line (but it is not advantageous)

Moving the pieces

Each player alternately moves a piece of their color A piece can only be moved to an adjacent square Pieces moved only horizontally or vertically (not diagonally) A line of size 3 allows you to capture the opponent's piece In this case, remove an opponent's piece of your choice Only one piece is removed per move (even if several lines are formed) Lines with more than 3 pieces cannot be formed A piece cannot return to the position of the previous move You can only return to that space in subsequent moves

End of the game

The game ends when one player cannot win For example, if you only have 2 pieces on the board Or if you cannot make a valid move The player who cannot win loses the game
